Anantha Padmanabha Swamy Temple
Historical Background:
  • Ancient Origin:
    Mentioned in ancient texts and Tamil literature, potentially dating back to the 6th century CE or earlier. Major renovations and expansions were undertaken in the 18th century by the Travancore Maharaja, Marthanda Varma.

  • Establishment:
    The current structure was renovated and expanded in the 18th century by the Travancore Maharaja, Marthanda Varma, who dedicated his kingdom to Lord Padmanabha and ruled as the deity's servant.
Architectural Features:
  • Gopuram:
    The templeâ€™s grand entrance tower, known as the gopuram, is a seven-tiered structure adorned with intricate sculptures and carvings depicting scenes from Hindu mythology.

  • Mandapam:
    The temple has a large pillared hall called the Mandapam, used for various rituals and ceremonies. The pillars are intricately carved and display the artistic excellence of the period.
Religious Significance:
  • Primary Deity and Divya Desam:
    Dedicated to Lord Vishnu in the form of Anantha Padmanabha, depicted in a reclining posture on the serpent Anantha. Recognized as one of the 108 Divya Desams, the principal centers of worship in the Vaishnavite tradition.

  • Spiritual Pilgrimage:
    The deity's reclining posture symbolizes eternal sleep and the balance of the cosmos. A vital pilgrimage site for devotees, especially those following the Vaishnavite tradition.
Food Practices:
  • Prasad Distribution:
    Sacred food offerings include various traditional South Indian dishes like payasam (a type of sweet rice pudding)

  • Nivedyam:
    Daily offerings to the deity, including rice, sweets, and fruits, which are later distributed to devotees. Festive Feasts: Special feasts and offerings during festivals like Panguni Uthiram and Alpashi Utsavam, featuring elaborate preparations.
Sabarimala
Festivals:
Mandala Pooja:
Devotees observe 41 days of austerity which involves strict celibacy,a vegetarian diet,and other disciplines. The pilgrims, after completing the Vratham,embark on the journey to Sabarimala and climb the 18 sacred steps to reach the sanctum.
Onam:
Celebrates the harvest festival and the mythical homecoming of King Mahabali. Special prayers and offerings. Although Sabarimala is not the primary focus during Onam, devotees visit to seek blessings.
Makaravilakku:
Celebrates the celestial event of the Makara Jyothi,a sacred light appearing on the Ponnambalamedu hill. The evening "Deeparadhana" and sighting of the Makara Jyothi are the highlights.
Religious significance

Lord Ayyappa: Dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, the temple symbolizes the unity of Shaivism and Vaishnavism, as Ayyappa is considered the son of Shiva and Mohini.
Pilgrimage: One of the largest annual pilgrimages globally, attracting millions of devotees, particularly during the Mandala Pooja and Makaravilakku seasons.

Architectural Features

Traditional Kerala Architecture: The temple structure follows traditional Kerala architectural styles, with a tiled roof, wooden carvings, and a gold-plated sanctum.
Sannidhanam: The main temple complex is known as Sannidhanam, surrounded by various other shrines, including those dedicated to Malikappurathamma and Ganapathi.

Food Practices

Prasadam: The temple offers sacred food, including "Aravana Payasam" and "Appam", which are considered divine blessings.
Annaprasadam: Free meals are provided to pilgrims at the temple complex, typically consisting of traditional Kerala dishes like rice, sambar, and vegetables.

Attukal Bhagavathy Temple
History:
The temple is dedicated to Goddess Kannaki, an incarnation of Parvati, and is associated with the legendary story of Kannaki, who is revered in the Tamil epic "Silappathikaram."The present structure has been developed over centuries, with significant contributions from local devotees and the Travancore royal family.
Architecture:
Traditional Kerala Style: The temple is built in the traditional Kerala architectural style, characterized by sloping tiled roofs, wooden carvings, and intricate murals.
Ornate Gopuram: The temple entrance is marked by a beautifully decorated gopuram (tower), featuring carvings of various deities and mythological scenes.
Festivals:
Attukal Pongala: The most famous festival, celebrated in February-March, where millions of women gather to prepare Pongala, a sweet offering made of rice, jaggery, and coconut.
Kuthiyottam:Boys aged between 10 and 12 are selected to perform rituals, believed to symbolize the sacrifice of a young warrior for the goddess.
Thottampattu: The festival features traditional songs and dances dedicated to the goddess.

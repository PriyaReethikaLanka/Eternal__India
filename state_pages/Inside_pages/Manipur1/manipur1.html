TEMPLES OF MANIPUR

SANAMAHI TEMPLE
Historical Background
  • Origins and Establishment:
    The Sanamahi Temple is dedicated to Lainingthou Sanamahi, one of the principal deities in the traditional Meitei religion known as Sanamahism. This indigenous faith predates the advent of Hinduism and Christianity in the region.

  • Significance in Meitei Religion:
    Lainingthou Sanamahi is regarded as the household deity and protector. Every traditional Meitei household typically has a sacred corner dedicated to Sanamahi.
Architectural Features
  • Traditional Meitei Architecture
    The temple’s design reflects traditional Meitei architecture, characterized by simplicity and a strong connection to nature.

  • Sanctum Sanctorum
    The sanctum sanctorum, or the inner shrine, is the most sacred part of the temple. It houses the idol of Lainingthou Sanamahi, the principal deity worshipped at the temple.
Religious Significance
  • Dedication to Lainingthou Sanamahi
    The temple is dedicated to Lainingthou Sanamahi, one of the principal deities in Sanamahism. Sanamahi is considered the household god, protector of the home, and a guardian deity who embodies the spiritual essence of the Meitei people.
  • Symbol of Sanamahism

    The Sanamahi Temple is a symbol of the Sanamahism faith, which predates the advent of Hinduism and Christianity in Manipur. It represents the cultural and religious identity of the Meitei people.
Food Practices
  • Prasadam
    Prasadam is food that has been offered to Lainingthou Sanamahi and is considered sacred. Devotees believe that consuming prasadam bestows divine blessings and spiritual merit.

  • Vegetarian Cuisine
    The food practices at the Sanamahi Temple are strictly vegetarian, in line with the principles of Sanamahism, which emphasize non-violence and purity.
RADHA KRISHNA CHANDRA ISKON
Festivals
Janmashtami
Janmashtami celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna. It is one of the most important festivals in the ISKCON calendar.Devotees fast until midnight, perform abhisheka (ceremonial bathing) of the deity, sing bhajans (devotional songs), and participate in kirtans.
Radhashtami
Radhashtami marks the appearance day of Radha, the eternal consort of Lord Krishna.The day involves special prayers, singing of Radha’s glories, and a grand abhisheka of Radha Krishna deities. Devotees also fast and prepare special offerings for the deities.
Religious significance

Focus on Bhakti: The temple is dedicated to Radha and Krishna, who are worshipped as the supreme divine couple representing pure love and devotion. Radha symbolizes the supreme devotee, and Krishna embodies the Supreme Personality of Godhead
Teachings of Lord Krishna: The teachings and practices at the temple are based on sacred texts such as the Bhagavad Gita and the Srimad Bhagavatam.

Architectural Features

Traditional Indian Temple Architecture:The temple architecture draws heavily from traditional Vedic design principles, incorporating elements from ancient Indian temple architecture.
Deity Sanctum: At the heart of the temple is the garbhagriha, or sanctum sanctorum, where the main deities, Radha and Krishna, are enshrined. This area is usually ornately decorated and is the focal point of all worship activities.

Food Practices

Prasadam Distribution: All food prepared in the temple kitchen is first offered to the deities of Radha and Krishna. This sanctified food, known as prasadam, is then distributed to devotees and visitors. It is considered to carry the blessings of the deities.
Vegetarianism: The temple adheres to a strict vegetarian diet, reflecting the principles of non-violence (ahimsa) and purity. No meat, fish, eggs, onions, or garlic are used in the food preparation.

SHRI GOVINDA TEMPLE
History:
The Shri Govindaji Temple was originally built in 1846 during the reign of Maharaja Nara Singh, who was the King of Manipur. The temple, however, gained prominence under Maharaja Chandrakirti, who renovated and expanded it. The construction of the temple marked a significant event in the spread of Vaishnavism in Manipur.
Architecture:
The Shri Govindaji Temple is designed with a central sanctum and subsidiary shrines, arranged within a large courtyard. The temple complex is spacious and well-planned, allowing for the accommodation of a large number of devotees during religious festivals and daily worship.
Festivals:
Janmashtami: The festival is observed with elaborate rituals, including midnight prayers and the recitation of devotional songs and hymns. The temple is beautifully decorated with flowers and lights.
Ratha Yatra: Ratha Yatra, also known as the Chariot Festival, is another major festival at Shri Govindaji Temple. This festival is dedicated to Lord Jagannath.
Ningol Chakouba: Ningol Chakouba is a traditional festival in Manipur, where married daughters are invited to their parental home for a special feast.

LET'S EXPLORE THE
TEMPLES OF GUJARAT

AKSHARDHAM
Historical Background
  • Ancient Origin:
    The construction of Akshardham Temple was initiated by the spiritual leader Pramukh Swami Maharaj, the fifth spiritual successor of Bhagwan Swaminarayan, the founder of the Swaminarayan Sampradaya.

  • Establishment:
    The temple complex was inaugurated on November 6, 2005, by the then-President of India, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam.
Architectural Features
  • Main Temple (Mandir)
    The main temple is built in a traditional Hindu architectural style with intricate carvings and stunning sculptures. It includes a central shrine housing the idol of Bhagwan Swaminarayan.

  • Intricate Carvings
    Sculptures: The walls, pillars, and ceilings of the temple are adorned with thousands of carved images depicting gods, goddesses, dancers, musicians, and animals.
Religious Significance
  • Dedication to Bhagwan Swaminarayan
    The temple is dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan, a revered spiritual leader and deity who is believed to be an incarnation of God by his followers.

  • Spiritual Practices
    The temple conducts daily rituals such as aarti, bhajans, and puja, which attract devotees who seek blessings and spiritual solace. Major Hindu festivals like Diwali, Janmashtami, and Swaminarayan Jayanti are celebrated with grandeur, drawing large crowds and fostering a sense of community.
Food Practices
  • Prasadam
    Food prepared in the temple is offered to the deities before being distributed to devotees. This blessed food, known as prasadam, is considered sacred and is believed to carry divine blessings.

  • Vegetarian Cuisine
    The temple follows a strict vegetarian diet, in accordance with Hindu dietary laws, promoting ahimsa (non-violence) and respect for all living beings.
SOMANATH TEMPLE
Festivals
Kutchh Desert
A major festival is held at Somnath during Mahashivratri which coincide with the Kutchh Desert festival held at the same time. Somnath also celebrates a large fair on the day of the full moon of Kartik Purnima in November/December.
Shivratri
Shivaratri is the birthday of Lord Shiva and celebrated on the 6th night of the dark Phalgun every year. Mahashivaratri marks the night when Lord Shiva performed the 'Tandava'. It is also believed that on this day Lord Shiva was married to Parvati Ma.

Religious significance

Jyotirlinga Shrine: One of the Twelve Jyotirlingas: The Somnath Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are considered the holiest Shiva shrines in Hinduism.
Spiritual Practices: The Somnath Temple is a major pilgrimage site, attracting millions of devotees from around the world. Pilgrims visit the temple to seek blessings, perform rituals, and offer prayers.

Architectural Features

Chalukya Style Architecture: The temple is built in the Chalukya style of architecture, also known as the "Kailash Mahameru Prasad" style.
Shikhar: At the top of the spire, there is a large kalash (pot) and a trident, which are symbolic elements in Hindu temple architecture. A statue of Nandi, the sacred bull of Lord Shiva, is positioned in front of the main sanctum.

Food Practices

Prasadam Distribution: Prasadam, which is food offered to the deity, holds great significance at the Somnath Temple. Devotees believe that consuming prasadam grants divine blessings and spiritual merit.
Festive Food Offerings: Common offerings include fruits, sweets, and traditional vegetarian dishes prepared with utmost purity and devotion.

Dwaraka
History:
The temple’s origins date back to the 16th century, though it was reconstructed in the early 20th century. It was initially built by the Hindu Mahant, but the temple underwent renovations and expansions over time.
The most significant renovation work was carried out in the early 20th century under the patronage of Maharaja Adhiraj of the princely state of Durgiana. The current structure reflects this period's architectural style and artistry.
Architecture:
The Durgiana Temple's architecture is inspired by the Golden Temple in Amritsar, reflecting a similar blend of marble and gold. The temple features a grand entrance, intricately carved marble, and a golden dome.
The main sanctum is surrounded by a large courtyard with several smaller shrines and a beautiful water tank, adding to the temple's serene ambiance. The design elements reflect traditional Hindu temple architecture with elaborate carvings and decorative motifs.
Festivals:
Navaratri: A major festival dedicated to the goddess Durga, celebrated with great fervor. The temple hosts special prayers, processions, and cultural performances during this period.
Diwali: The festival of lights is also celebrated here, with the temple beautifully illuminated and special ceremonies held to honor the goddess.
Vaisakhi: This festival, marking the harvest season and the formation of the Khalsa, is celebrated with enthusiasm, including prayers and community activities.

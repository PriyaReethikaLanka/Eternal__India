TEMPLES OF MEGHALAYA

Nartiang Durga Temple
Historical Background
  • Ancient Origins:
    The Nartiang Durga Temple is believed to have been established in the 16th century during the reign of the Jaintia kings. The Jaintia Kingdom, also known as the Sutnga Kingdom, was a powerful and influential kingdom in the northeastern region of India.

  • Royal Patronage:
    The temple gained prominence under the patronage of the Jaintia kings, particularly King Dhan Manik, who was a devout follower of Goddess Durga.
Architectural Features
  • Style and Design
    The Nartiang Durga Temple showcases a blend of Hindu and Khasi architectural styles. The temple’s design is simple yet profound, reflecting the indigenous Khasi influence.

  • Sanctum Sanctorum
    he sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Goddess Durga. Unlike typical idols, the representation of Durga here is a simple stone slab or pillar, which is worshipped as the goddess.
Religious Significance
  • Shakti Peetha
    The Nartiang Durga Temple is believed to be one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, which are sacred shrines where parts of the goddess Sati's body are said to have fallen during Lord Shiva's Tandava (cosmic dance) with her corpse.
  • Matrika Worship

    The temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga, a form of Shakti or the divine feminine power. Devotees worship her as the Mother Goddess, who embodies strength, power, and protection.
Food Practices
  • Prasadam
    As in many Hindu temples, food offerings are an integral part of the worship at the Nartiang Durga Temple. Devotees often bring fruits, sweets, and other food items as offerings to the goddess.

  • Community Feasts
    These community feasts, known as Bhandara in some regions, typically include traditional vegetarian dishes like rice, lentils, vegetables, and sweets.
